Social Determinants of Health

A fundamental analysis of the relationship between socioeconomic factors and population health

Data Description

The data was sourced from Country Health Rankings. It includes health, economic, and demographic characteristics measured at the county level in the US. There are a total of 25 columns of data from the following categories:

  • Data identifiers
    • An additional column "Region" was created based on the "State Abbreviation" column.
  • Outcome variables
    • The outcome variable I chose to examine was Poor Health, which indicates the proportion of county that has a poor health status
  • Social determinants of health
    • Behavioral
    • Environmental
    • Demographic

Objective

Looking through the data, I set out three guiding questions that oriented my analyses:

  1. Which social determinants of health is the most significant predictor of the health index?
  2. How does socioeconomic level affect the health index?
  3. How does access/quality of care impact the health index?

Highlights of Relevant Findings

In the South and Midwest regions, the number of smokers and excessive drinkers are negatively correlated, indicating that cigarettes and alcohol are substitute goods - people choose one over the other. On the other hand, in the West region, smokers and excessive drinks are positively correlated, indicating that they are complimentary goods - people who tend to drink also tend to smoke and vice versa.

Unsurprisingly, counties with more income inequality or food insecurity have higher proportion of people with poor health

Predictive Modeling for Health Index

A decision tree is performed using all behavioral variables along with "Food Insecurity Quintile" and "Income Inequality Quartile" variables from the environmental category. "Proportion.of.Smokers" has the highest variable importance, meaning that smoking is the most important factor of poor health. Generally, the counties that have lower health index are the ones that has:

  • A proportion of smokers greater than 19.1%
  • A proportion of excessive drinkers less than 15%
  • A proportion of insufficient sleepers greater than 41.4%

Suggestions for Future Research

  • Expanding "Social Determinants of Health - Environmental" to include health-related policy such as beverage tax and cigarette tax, as well as physical determinants such as climate/weather.
  • Examining the change of health index over time
  • Conduct more/bigger regression models
      • Add interaction terms (ex. interaction between drinks and smokers variables)
