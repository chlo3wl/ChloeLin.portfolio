Hyperlocal Icons

Inspired by Susan Kare known as the “woman who gave the Macintosh a smile” for designing the distinctive icons, typefaces, and other graphic elements that gave the Apple Macintosh its characteristic—and widely emulated—look and feel.

A series of icons embodying the past, present, and future that spawn from the pill capsule (icon 1).

The origin of pills traces back to traditional herbal medicines (icon 2), which is often grounded and mixed in the mortar and pestle (icon 5). With the outbreaks of epidemics and pandemics throughout the history of civilization, the first relatively effective vaccine was produced for influenza around 1945. Vaccines have since then become an effective preventive measure against viruses.

Bringing the capsule back to the present, the evolution of medicine introduces antibody solutions contained in an injection syringe (icon 4). At the same time, the modern practice of medicine is tethered with negative health implications, the more severe examples include the opioid crisis. The addition of the fuse to the pill capsule (icon 3) attempts to capture the dangerous nature of rapid pharmaceutical breakthroughs.

The future is depicted by two juxtaposing icons, one filled with optimism and the other tamed by corruption. Icon 7 advocates for a form of “natural remedies” to “cure” the environmentally deteriorating Earth. Contrastingly, icon 8 alludes to the institutional corruption of today’s pharmaceutical industry. Together, these two icons forecast alternative paths for the future.
